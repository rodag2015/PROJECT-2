"I'm a huge fan of Fromsoft and was beyond hyped for Elden Ring. Fortunately, this game lived up to the hype as it successfully evolved not only the Souls formula but the open world of The Legend Of Zelda: Breath Of The Wild. The open world in this game is close to perfection in my opinion as it is not made up of FOMO style content but genuinely worthwhile sights, quests, and thrills that can be indulged in by the player at whatever pace or manner they want. Furthermore, it improves upon BOTW's world by having multiple handcrafted dungeons that greatly vary in scale, design, and necessity (i.e., the majority are optional) but are all uniformly excellent as a result of some truly incredible level and enemy design. In typical Fromsoft fashion, the combat is also fantastic with lots of depth and satisfying mechanics to play around with. The excellent combat is further complimented by great build variety with many cool weapons, magic, incantations, and ashes of war to try out. My only complaint would be that the boss fights are a bit of a mixed bag with a select few matching From's best, many being quite middling, and some being incredibly unpleasant especially during the late game. However, this is a minor complaint because Elden Ring is a triumph of game design and the rare case where a game manages to cement itself as an instant classic."-Ryan Lyster
